# 🧬 Sandbox Non-Reduction Guardrails

## Purpose

The customer sandbox must remain **multi-layered** without reducing the Elyria Systems method into a flat checklist, generic score, or exposed internal engine.

The sandbox is a **proof surface**. It is not the protected substrate.

---

## What the Sandbox Shows

The sandbox may show the customer:

1. **Dependency intake**
2. **Boundary classification**
3. **Decision outcome**
4. **Receipt generation**
5. **Replay and revalidation triggers**
6. **Board-facing buyer rules**

---

## What the Sandbox Must Not Show

The sandbox must not expose:

- protected kernel logic
- proprietary consequence-boundary substrate
- unpublished mathematical structure
- internal enforcement architecture
- hidden review heuristics
- private research corpus
- real customer evidence
- production secrets or credentials
- enough method detail to clone the full Elyria Systems stack

---

## Non-Reduction Rule

Do not describe the sandbox as only:

- a checklist
- a form
- a risk score
- a dashboard
- a compliance questionnaire
- a generic governance tool

Correct description:

**A customer-facing proof surface for testing whether an AI dependency is becoming load-bearing before authority, retention, jurisdiction, reversibility, exit, receipt, replay, and consequence exposure are proven.**

---

## Layer Shape

```text
customer sandbox surface
→ dependency intake
→ boundary grid
→ decision classification
→ receipt
→ replay / revalidation
→ board packet
→ protected substrate remains sealed
```

---

## Operating Rule

Expose enough for the customer to understand, test, and buy.

Do not expose enough for a competitor to reconstruct the protected system.
